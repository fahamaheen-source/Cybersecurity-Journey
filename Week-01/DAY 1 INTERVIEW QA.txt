CYBERSECURITY FUNDAMENTALS

Q: What is Cybersecurity?
A: Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, systems, and data from cyber attacks and unauthorized access.
________________________________________
Q. Why is Cybersecurity important?
Answer:
Cybersecurity is important because it protects sensitive data, prevents cyber attacks, reduces financial losses, and ensures business continuity.
________________________________________
Q. What can happen if a company does not have proper cybersecurity?
Answer:
The company may experience data breaches, financial losses, service disruptions, and reputation damage.
________________________________________
Q. How does cybersecurity help businesses?
Answer:
Cybersecurity protects business assets, customer information, and company operations from cyber threats.
________________________________________
Q. Why do companies need cybersecurity?
Answer:
Companies need cybersecurity to protect sensitive data, prevent cyber attacks, reduce financial losses, and ensure business continuity.
________________________________________
Q. What assets do companies protect using cybersecurity?
Answer:
Companies protect data, systems, networks, applications, and business reputation.
________________________________________
Q. What is business continuity?
Answer:
Business continuity means keeping business operations running normally even during security incidents or cyber attacks.
________________________________________
Q. Name some common cyber attacks.
Answer: Phishing, Malware, Ransomware, Password Attacks, DoS, DDoS, MITM, SQL Injection, XSS, and Insider Threats.
________________________________________Q. What is the difference between DoS and DDoS?
Answer: DoS uses a single source to attack a target, while DDoS uses multiple compromised systems.
________________________________________
Q. What is an Insider Threat?
Answer: An insider threat is a security risk originating from an organization's employees, contractors, or trusted individuals.
________________________________________
Q. What is the CIA Triad?
Answer:
The CIA Triad is a cybersecurity model consisting of Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability used to protect information and systems.
________________________________________
Q. What is Confidentiality?
Answer:
Confidentiality ensures that information is accessible only to authorized users.
________________________________________
Q. What is Integrity?
Answer:
Integrity ensures that data remains accurate and is not modified without authorization.
________________________________________
Q. What is Availability?
Answer:
Availability ensures that systems and data are accessible whenever needed by authorized users.
________________________________________
Q. Why is the CIA Triad important?
Answer:
The CIA Triad provides the foundation of information security by protecting data confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
________________________________________
Q. What is a Threat?
Answer:
A threat is anything that has the potential to cause harm to systems, networks, applications, or data.
________________________________________
Q. What is a Vulnerability?
Answer:
A vulnerability is a weakness that can be exploited by a threat.
________________________________________
Q. What is Risk?
Answer:
Risk is the possibility of a threat exploiting a vulnerability and causing damage.
________________________________________
Q. Give an example of Threat, Vulnerability, and Risk.
Answer:
Threat → Hacker
Vulnerability → Weak Password
Risk → Unauthorized Access
________________________________________
Q. What is the relationship between Threat, Vulnerability, and Risk?
Answer:
Risk occurs when a threat exploits a vulnerability and causes harm.
________________________________________
Q. Is a weak password a Threat or Vulnerability?
Answer:
A weak password is a Vulnerability because it is a weakness that can be exploited.
________________________________________
Q. Is malware a Threat or Vulnerability?
Answer:
Malware is a Threat because it can cause harm to systems and data.
________________________________________
What are Security Controls?
Security controls are safeguards used to protect systems and data from cyber threats.
________________________________________
What are the three types of Security Controls?
Preventive, Detective, and Corrective.
________________________________________
Give examples of Preventive Controls.
Firewall, Antivirus, MFA, Password Policies.
________________________________________
Give examples of Detective Controls.
SIEM, IDS, Security Logs.
________________________________________
Give examples of Corrective Controls.
Backups, Patch Management, Incident Response.
________________________________________
What is SIEM?
SIEM is a platform that collects and analyzes logs from different systems to detect security threats.
________________________________________
Is a Firewall Preventive or Detective?
Preventive.
________________________________________
Is SIEM Preventive or Detective?
Detective.


SOC FUNDAMENTALS INTERVIEW QUESTIONS
Q1. What is a SOC?
Answer:
A SOC (Security Operations Center) is a centralized unit that monitors, detects, investigates, and responds to cybersecurity threats and incidents in real time.
________________________________________
Q2. What is the purpose of a SOC?
Answer:
The purpose of a SOC is to protect an organization's systems and data, detect threats early, respond to incidents quickly, and maintain continuous security monitoring.
________________________________________
Q3. Why do organizations need a SOC?
Answer:
Organizations need a SOC to detect cyber attacks, reduce damage, protect sensitive data, maintain business continuity, and improve security visibility.
________________________________________
Q4. What does a SOC Analyst do?
Answer:
A SOC Analyst monitors security alerts, investigates suspicious activities, analyzes logs, detects threats, responds to incidents, and documents findings.
________________________________________
Q5. What are the responsibilities of a SOC Analyst?
Answer:
•	Monitoring security events 
•	Investigating alerts 
•	Analyzing logs 
•	Detecting threats 
•	Responding to incidents 
•	Escalating serious incidents 
•	Documentation and reporting 
________________________________________
SOC TIERS
Q6. What is a Tier 1 SOC Analyst?
Answer:
A Tier 1 Analyst is an entry-level SOC analyst responsible for monitoring alerts, performing basic investigations, validating alerts, and escalating incidents when necessary.
________________________________________
Q7. What is a Tier 2 SOC Analyst?
Answer:
A Tier 2 Analyst performs deeper investigations, analyzes multiple data sources, identifies attack types, and handles incident response activities.
________________________________________
Q8. What is a Tier 3 SOC Analyst?
Answer:
A Tier 3 Analyst is an advanced security professional responsible for threat hunting, malware analysis, detection engineering, and improving SOC security.
________________________________________
Q9. Difference between Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3?
Answer:
Tier	Main Responsibility
Tier 1	Monitoring & Alert Validation
Tier 2	Investigation & Incident Response
Tier 3	Threat Hunting & Security Improvement
________________________________________
Q10. What does a SOC Manager do?
Answer:
A SOC Manager supervises SOC operations, manages analysts, ensures incidents are handled properly, and improves SOC performance.
________________________________________
SOC WORKFLOW
Q11. Explain the SOC Workflow.
Answer:
The SOC Workflow is:
Alert → Triage → Investigation → Escalation → Response → Documentation → Post-Incident Review
________________________________________
Q12. What is Alert Triage?
Answer:
Alert Triage is the process of reviewing an alert, determining its severity, and deciding whether it is a real threat or a false positive.
________________________________________
Q13. What is a False Positive?
Answer:
A false positive is a security alert that appears malicious but is actually legitimate activity.
________________________________________
Q14. What is Incident Response?
Answer:
Incident Response is the process of containing, investigating, and recovering from a security incident.
________________________________________
Q15. Why is Documentation important in a SOC?
Answer:
Documentation helps track incidents, record actions taken, support investigations, and improve future security operations.
________________________________________

SOC TOOLS

Answer:
SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) is a security solution that collects, analyzes, and correlates logs from multiple sources to detect threats.
________________________________________

•	IBM QRadar 
________________________________________
Q20. What is EDR?
Answer:
EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response) is a security solution that monitors endpoint devices and helps detect and respond to threats.
