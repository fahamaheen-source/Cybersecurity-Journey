WHAT IS CYBERSECURITY?
Notes
•	Cybersecurity protects computers, networks, systems, and data from cyber attacks.
•	It prevents unauthorized access, theft, and damage.
•	Common attacks include phishing, malware, and ransomware.
Why Cybersecurity Matters
•	Protects sensitive information.
•	Prevents cyber attacks.
•	Reduces financial losses.
•	Maintains customer trust.
•	Protects company reputation.
•	Ensures business continuity.
Consequences of Poor Security
•	Data breaches
•	Financial loss
•	Service disruption
•	Legal issues
•	Reputation damage
Key Point
Cybersecurity is important because it protects an organization's data, systems, employees, and customers from cyber threats.

Why Companies Need Security
•	Protect customer data.
•	Protect employee information.
•	Prevent cyber attacks.
•	Reduce financial losses.
•	Maintain customer trust.
•	Meet compliance and legal requirements.
•	Ensure business continuity.
What Companies Protect
•	Data
•	Systems
•	Networks
•	Applications
•	Business reputation
Key Point
Companies need security to protect their assets, customers, employees, and business operations from cyber threats.

Common Cyber Attacks
1. Phishing
•	A social engineering attack that uses fake emails, messages, or websites.
•	Goal: Steal usernames, passwords, bank details, or sensitive information.
Example: Fake bank email asking you to log in.
________________________________________
2. Malware
•	Malicious software designed to damage systems or steal data.
Types:
•	Virus
•	Worm
•	Trojan
Example: Downloading infected software.
________________________________________
3. Ransomware
•	A type of malware that encrypts files and demands payment.
•	Can stop business operations.
Example: Company files become inaccessible until ransom is paid.
________________________________________
4. Password Attack
•	Attempts to steal or guess passwords.
Examples:
•	Brute Force Attack
•	Dictionary Attack
Goal: Gain unauthorized access to accounts.
________________________________________
5. Denial of Service (DoS)
•	Attacker floods a system with traffic.
•	Makes services unavailable to legitimate users.
Example: Website becomes inaccessible.
________________________________________
6. Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)
•	Similar to DoS but uses many infected devices.
•	Much more powerful than a normal DoS attack.
Example: Thousands of compromised computers attacking one website.
________________________________________
7. Man-in-the-Middle (MITM)
•	Attacker secretly intercepts communication between two parties.
Example: Stealing data on an insecure public Wi-Fi network.
________________________________________
8. SQL Injection
•	Attacker inserts malicious SQL commands into an application.
•	Used to access or modify database information.
Example: Stealing customer records from a website database.
________________________________________
9. Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)
•	Attacker injects malicious scripts into a website.
•	Affects website users.
Example: Stealing user session cookies.
________________________________________
10. Insider Threat
•	Threat originating from within the organization.
•	Can be intentional or accidental.
Example: Employee leaking company data.
CIA Triad
The CIA Triad is the core model of information security consisting of Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability.
________________________________________
1. Confidentiality
Definition:
Ensures that information is accessible only to authorized users.
Examples:
•	Password-protected accounts
•	Encrypted data
•	MFA
Goal:
Prevent unauthorized access.
________________________________________
2. Integrity
Definition:
Ensures that data remains accurate, complete, and unmodified.
Examples:
•	Student marks database
•	Financial records
Goal:
Prevent unauthorized modification of data.
________________________________________
3. Availability
Definition:
Ensures that systems and data are available whenever needed.
Examples:
•	Banking websites
•	Hospital systems
Goal:
Ensure uninterrupted access to services.
________________________________________
Why CIA Triad is Important
•	Protects sensitive information.
•	Maintains data accuracy.
•	Ensures systems remain accessible.
•	Forms the foundation of cybersecurity.
________________________________________
Key Point
Cybersecurity aims to maintain:
•	Confidentiality
•	Integrity
•	Availability
of information and systems.

THREATS, VULNERABILITIES & RISKS
Threat
Definition:
A threat is anything that has the potential to cause harm to systems, networks, applications, or data.
Examples:
•	Hacker
•	Malware
•	Ransomware
•	Phishing
•	Insider Threat
________________________________________
Vulnerability
Definition:
A vulnerability is a weakness that can be exploited by a threat.
Examples:
•	Weak Password
•	Unpatched System
•	Outdated Software
•	Misconfigured Firewall
________________________________________
Risk
Definition:
Risk is the possibility of a threat exploiting a vulnerability and causing damage.
Examples:
•	Data Breach
•	Financial Loss
•	Service Disruption
•	Unauthorized Access
________________________________________
Relationship
Threat + Vulnerability = Risk
________________________________________
Example
Threat → Hacker
Vulnerability → Weak Password
Risk → Account Compromise
________________________________________
Why SOC Analysts Care
•	Identify threats
•	Detect vulnerabilities
•	Reduce risks
•	Protect organizational assets

TYPES OF SECURITY CONTROLS
Security Controls
Security controls are safeguards used to protect systems, networks, applications, and data from cyber threats.
________________________________________
Preventive Controls
Purpose: Prevent attacks before they occur.
Examples:
•	Firewall
•	Antivirus
•	MFA
•	Strong Passwords
•	Access Control
________________________________________
Detective Controls
Purpose: Detect attacks and suspicious activities.
Examples:
•	SIEM
•	IDS
•	Security Logs
•	Monitoring Tools
________________________________________
Corrective Controls
Purpose: Recover from incidents and reduce damage.
Examples:
•	Backups
•	Patch Management
•	Incident Response
•	Disaster Recovery
________________________________________
Important Security Tools
Firewall
Blocks unauthorized network traffic.
Antivirus
Detects and removes malware.
MFA
Requires multiple authentication factors.
SIEM
Collects and analyzes security logs to detect threats.
